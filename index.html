Chameleon

  1. Chameleons have eyes in the backs of their heads.
  2. Most chameleons eat on things you wouldn’t want to find on your dinner plate.
  3. Chameleons take a while to hatch, and don’t live very long.


Pigeon

  1. Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  2. Pigeons mate for life, and tend to raise two chicks at the same time.
  3. Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet, and at an average speed of 77.6 mph.


Camel

  1. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  2. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.
  3. Some calves are born completely white and turn brown as their adult coat comes in.


Teddy Bear

  1. The teddy was named after President Theodore Roosevelt, after he refused to shoot a bear during a 1902 hunting trip.
  2. The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
  3. The official term for a teddy bear collector is an ‘arctophile’.

Panda

  1. Pandas go from pink to white and black.
  2. An adult panda can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!
  3. Speaking of bamboo, pandas have six fingers on each paw to help them eat bamboo more quickly and efficiently.

Flying Cicada

  1. The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.
  2. Female cicada may be attracted to the sound of motors!
  3. The body of a cicada is similar to that of a violin.

Hippo

  2. One of the closest relatives to the hippo are whales.
  3. A hippo's skin is bulletproof.
